# Beginner Full Body — 3 Days Per Week

A 4-week program for complete beginners. Focus on form, not intensity. The goal is to build the habit of training consistently 3 times per week.

## Schedule
- Day 1: Full Body A (Monday)
- Day 2: Rest
- Day 3: Full Body B (Wednesday)
- Day 4: Rest
- Day 5: Full Body C (Friday)
- Day 6-7: Rest or light walk

## Week 1-2: Learn the Movements

### Full Body A
**Warm-up (5 min):** Jumping jacks 30s, arm circles 30s, leg swings 30s each, bodyweight squats 10x

1. **Bodyweight Squat** — 3 sets x 10 reps, 60s rest
   - Form: Feet shoulder-width, chest up, sit back like sitting in a chair
   - Easier: Squat to a chair (box squat)
   - Harder: Add pause at bottom (3 seconds)

2. **Push-up** (knees or full) — 3 sets x 8 reps, 60s rest
   - Form: Hands under shoulders, body straight, lower chest to floor
   - Easier: Wall push-ups or incline push-ups on a table
   - Harder: Full push-ups with slow negative (3 seconds down)

3. **Dumbbell Row** (or bodyweight row) — 3 sets x 10 each arm, 60s rest
   - Form: Flat back, pull to hip, squeeze shoulder blade
   - Easier: Bodyweight row using a table edge
   - Harder: Increase dumbbell weight

4. **Plank** — 3 sets x 20-30 seconds, 45s rest
   - Form: Elbows under shoulders, body straight, squeeze core
   - Easier: Knees on ground
   - Harder: Extend to 45 seconds

5. **Glute Bridge** — 3 sets x 12 reps, 45s rest
   - Form: Feet flat, drive through heels, squeeze glutes at top
   - Easier: Shorter range of motion
   - Harder: Single-leg glute bridge

**Cool-down (5 min):** Quad stretch 30s each, hamstring stretch 30s each, chest stretch 30s, child's pose 60s

### Full Body B
**Warm-up (5 min):** High knees 30s, hip circles 30s, shoulder rolls 30s, inchworms 5x

1. **Reverse Lunge** — 3 sets x 8 each leg, 60s rest
   - Form: Step back, both knees 90 degrees, front knee over ankle
   - Easier: Hold onto a wall for balance
   - Harder: Add dumbbells

2. **Dumbbell Overhead Press** (or pike push-up) — 3 sets x 8 reps, 60s rest
   - Form: Press straight up, arms fully extended, core tight
   - Easier: Seated press or wall pike push-up
   - Harder: Increase weight or strict pike push-up

3. **Superman Hold** — 3 sets x 10 reps (2s hold each), 45s rest
   - Form: Lying face down, lift arms and legs simultaneously
   - Easier: Lift arms only, then legs only
   - Harder: 5-second holds

4. **Dead Bug** — 3 sets x 8 each side, 45s rest
   - Form: Back flat on floor, extend opposite arm and leg
   - Easier: Just lower legs, arms stay up
   - Harder: Slower movement (5 seconds per rep)

5. **Wall Sit** — 3 sets x 20-30 seconds, 45s rest
   - Form: Back flat against wall, thighs parallel to floor
   - Easier: Higher position (less bend)
   - Harder: 45 seconds or hold a weight

**Cool-down (5 min):** Hip flexor stretch 30s each, calf stretch 30s each, shoulder stretch 30s each, deep breathing 60s

### Full Body C
**Warm-up (5 min):** Jumping jacks 30s, arm swings 30s, bodyweight squats 10x, push-up 5x

1. **Goblet Squat** (or bodyweight squat) — 3 sets x 10 reps, 60s rest
   - Form: Hold weight at chest, squat deep, elbows inside knees
   - Easier: Bodyweight squat
   - Harder: Add pause, increase weight

2. **Incline Push-up** (or full push-up) — 3 sets x 10 reps, 60s rest
   - Form: Hands on bench/step, same push-up form
   - Easier: Higher incline (wall)
   - Harder: Flat push-ups or decline

3. **Dumbbell Deadlift** (or single-leg RDL) — 3 sets x 10 reps, 60s rest
   - Form: Hinge at hips, flat back, weights close to legs
   - Easier: Bodyweight hip hinge
   - Harder: Increase weight

4. **Bird Dog** — 3 sets x 8 each side, 45s rest
   - Form: On all fours, extend opposite arm and leg, hold 2s
   - Easier: Just arm or just leg
   - Harder: 5-second holds

5. **Calf Raises** — 3 sets x 15 reps, 30s rest
   - Form: Rise onto toes, squeeze at top, slow descent
   - Easier: Both feet, hold wall for balance
   - Harder: Single-leg calf raises

**Cool-down (5 min):** Full body stretch — hamstrings, quads, hip flexors, chest, shoulders, 30s each

## Week 3-4: Increase Volume

Same exercises, but:
- Add 1-2 reps per set to every exercise
- Increase plank/wall sit by 10 seconds
- If using weights, add 1-2 kg
- Try the "harder" variation for 1-2 exercises you feel confident in

## Progression
After 4 weeks, if you can complete all exercises with good form:
- Move to 4-day upper/lower split
- Start adding more weight progressively
- You've built the habit — now build the strength
