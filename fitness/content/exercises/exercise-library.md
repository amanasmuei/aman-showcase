# Exercise Library

Quick reference for common exercises with form cues and variations.

## Lower Body

### Squat (Bodyweight / Goblet / Barbell)
- **Muscles:** Quads, glutes, core
- **Form:** Feet shoulder-width, chest up, knees track over toes, depth to parallel
- **Easier:** Box squat (squat to chair), wall squat
- **Harder:** Barbell back squat, front squat, pause squat

### Lunge (Forward / Reverse / Walking)
- **Muscles:** Quads, glutes, hamstrings, balance
- **Form:** Big step, both knees 90 degrees, front knee over ankle
- **Easier:** Stationary lunge holding wall, shorter range of motion
- **Harder:** Walking lunges with dumbbells, Bulgarian split squat

### Deadlift (Dumbbell / Barbell / Romanian)
- **Muscles:** Hamstrings, glutes, lower back, grip
- **Form:** Hinge at hips, flat back, weight close to body, drive through heels
- **Easier:** Bodyweight hip hinge, kettlebell deadlift
- **Harder:** Barbell conventional deadlift, single-leg RDL

### Glute Bridge / Hip Thrust
- **Muscles:** Glutes, hamstrings
- **Form:** Feet flat, drive through heels, squeeze glutes at top, hold 1-2s
- **Easier:** Both feet, shorter range
- **Harder:** Single-leg, barbell hip thrust, elevated feet

### Calf Raise
- **Muscles:** Calves (gastrocnemius, soleus)
- **Form:** Rise onto toes, full squeeze at top, slow 3s descent
- **Easier:** Both feet, hold wall
- **Harder:** Single-leg, add weight, off a step for full range

## Upper Body — Push

### Push-up (Wall / Incline / Full / Decline)
- **Muscles:** Chest, shoulders, triceps, core
- **Form:** Hands under shoulders, body straight line, elbows 45 degrees
- **Easier:** Wall push-ups → incline → knee push-ups
- **Harder:** Full → decline → diamond → weighted

### Overhead Press (Dumbbell / Barbell / Pike Push-up)
- **Muscles:** Shoulders, triceps, upper chest
- **Form:** Press straight up, arms fully extended, core braced
- **Easier:** Seated press, lighter weight
- **Harder:** Strict press, push press, handstand push-up

### Dip (Bench / Parallel Bars)
- **Muscles:** Chest, triceps, shoulders
- **Form:** Lower until upper arms parallel, press up, lean slightly forward for chest
- **Easier:** Bench dips with bent knees
- **Harder:** Parallel bar dips, weighted dips

## Upper Body — Pull

### Row (Dumbbell / Barbell / Bodyweight)
- **Muscles:** Back (lats, rhomboids), biceps, rear delts
- **Form:** Flat back, pull to hip/lower chest, squeeze shoulder blades
- **Easier:** Bodyweight row using table, light dumbbells
- **Harder:** Barbell row, heavy dumbbell row, chest-supported row

### Pull-up / Chin-up
- **Muscles:** Lats, biceps, grip, core
- **Form:** Full hang, pull chin over bar, control descent
- **Easier:** Negative pull-ups (jump up, slow lower), band-assisted
- **Harder:** Weighted pull-ups, L-sit pull-ups

### Face Pull / Band Pull-apart
- **Muscles:** Rear delts, upper back, rotator cuff
- **Form:** Pull to face, elbows high, squeeze shoulder blades
- **Easier:** Light band
- **Harder:** Heavy band, cable face pulls

## Core

### Plank (Forearm / High / Side)
- **Muscles:** Core, shoulders, glutes
- **Form:** Body straight line, squeeze everything, breathe normally
- **Easier:** Knee plank
- **Harder:** Extended plank, plank with shoulder taps

### Dead Bug
- **Muscles:** Deep core, hip flexors
- **Form:** Back flat on floor, extend opposite arm and leg alternately
- **Easier:** Just legs
- **Harder:** Hold weight, slower tempo

### Bird Dog
- **Muscles:** Core, lower back, glutes
- **Form:** All fours, extend opposite arm and leg, hold 2-3s
- **Easier:** Just arm or just leg
- **Harder:** Add ankle weights, longer holds

### Superman / Back Extension
- **Muscles:** Lower back, glutes, upper back
- **Form:** Face down, lift arms and legs off floor simultaneously
- **Easier:** Alternating arm/leg lifts
- **Harder:** Hold for 5s, add light weight

## Cardio

### Walking — 30-60 min, low intensity, daily if possible
### Jogging — 20-30 min, moderate intensity, 2-3x per week
### HIIT — 15-20 min, high intensity intervals, 1-2x per week (not for beginners)
### Jump Rope — 10-15 min, great for coordination and conditioning
### Cycling — 30-45 min, low impact, good for active recovery
