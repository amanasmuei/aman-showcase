# Nutrition Basics

Simple, sustainable nutrition guidelines — not a strict diet.

## Core Principles
- Eat mostly whole foods: vegetables, fruits, lean proteins, whole grains, healthy fats
- Protein at every meal: chicken, fish, eggs, beans, tofu, dairy
- Drink water: aim for 2-3 liters daily, more during training
- Don't ban any food: it's about balance, not restriction
- Consistency over perfection: 80% good choices is enough

## For Your Goals

### Lose Fat
- Moderate calorie deficit: eat ~300-500 calories below maintenance
- High protein: 1.8-2.2g per kg bodyweight (preserves muscle)
- Don't drop below: 1200 cal (women) / 1500 cal (men)
- Rate: 0.5-1kg per week is healthy and sustainable
- Tip: fill half your plate with vegetables — they fill you up for few calories

### Build Muscle
- Slight calorie surplus: eat ~300-500 calories above maintenance
- High protein: 1.6-2.2g per kg bodyweight
- Carbs are fuel: don't cut them — you need energy to train hard
- Eat within 2 hours after training (protein + carbs)
- Rate: 0.5-1kg per month of lean muscle is realistic

### General Health
- Balanced meals: protein + carbs + vegetables + healthy fat
- Eat the rainbow: variety of colored vegetables and fruits
- Limit processed food, sugary drinks, excessive alcohol
- Listen to your body: eat when hungry, stop when satisfied

## Simple Meal Ideas

### High Protein Breakfasts
- Eggs (scrambled/boiled) + whole grain toast + fruit
- Greek yogurt + granola + berries
- Oatmeal + protein powder + banana + peanut butter

### Balanced Lunches
- Grilled chicken + rice + vegetables
- Tuna salad + whole grain bread + mixed greens
- Lentil soup + bread + side salad

### Healthy Dinners
- Salmon + sweet potato + broccoli
- Chicken stir-fry + brown rice + mixed vegetables
- Bean chili + rice + avocado

### Snacks
- Apple + peanut butter
- Boiled eggs
- Mixed nuts + dried fruit
- Protein shake + banana

## Hydration Guide
- Morning: 500ml upon waking
- Before workout: 500ml (1-2 hours before)
- During workout: 200-300ml every 15-20 minutes
- After workout: 500ml within 30 minutes
- Throughout day: sip regularly, don't wait until thirsty

## What to Avoid
- Fad diets (keto, carnivore, etc. for beginners — these are advanced tools)
- "Detox" teas and cleanses — your liver and kidneys already detox
- Extreme caloric restriction — it backfires (metabolism slows, muscle loss)
- Overthinking macros as a beginner — eat whole foods and enough protein first
