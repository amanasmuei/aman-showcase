# Personal Fitness Training

## Approach
Build sustainable fitness habits through progressive overload, consistency, and enjoyment. Meet people where they are — a complete beginner and an experienced lifter need completely different programs. Focus on compound movements that give the most results for time invested. Make fitness feel achievable, not overwhelming.

## Key Principles
- Progressive overload: gradually increase weight, reps, or intensity over time
- Compound movements first: squat, deadlift, bench press, overhead press, row, pull-up
- Train each muscle group 2x per week for optimal growth
- Rest 48-72 hours between training the same muscle group
- Sleep 7-9 hours — it's when muscles actually grow
- Deload every 4-6 weeks: reduce intensity by 40-50% for recovery

## Program Design
- Beginners (0-6 months): 3 days/week full-body workouts, bodyweight → light weights
- Intermediate (6-18 months): 4 days/week upper/lower split, progressive overload focus
- Advanced (18+ months): 5-6 days/week push/pull/legs or specialized programs
- Cardio: 150 min moderate or 75 min vigorous per week (WHO recommendation)
- Flexibility: 10 min stretching after every workout, yoga 1-2x per week optional

## Common Mistakes to Watch
- Skipping warm-up — increases injury risk significantly
- Too much weight too fast — ego lifting leads to injury
- Only training "mirror muscles" (chest, biceps) — ignoring back, legs, core
- Not eating enough protein — aim for 1.6-2.2g per kg bodyweight
- Inconsistent training — 3x per week consistently beats 6x per week for 2 weeks then quitting
- Comparing to others — focus on personal progress only

## Exercise Form Cues (Key Movements)
- Squat: feet shoulder-width, chest up, knees track over toes, depth to parallel or below
- Push-up: hands under shoulders, body straight line, elbows 45 degrees, full range of motion
- Deadlift: bar over mid-foot, back flat, drive through heels, squeeze glutes at top
- Plank: elbows under shoulders, body straight, squeeze core, breathe normally
- Row: pull to lower chest, squeeze shoulder blades together, control the negative

## Nutrition Basics
- Calorie surplus for muscle gain (~300-500 above maintenance)
- Calorie deficit for fat loss (~300-500 below maintenance)
- Protein: 1.6-2.2g per kg bodyweight
- Carbs: fuel for training — don't cut them too low
- Fats: essential for hormones — minimum 0.5g per kg bodyweight
- Water: 2-3 liters daily, more during intense training

## Level Guidelines
- Lv.1 (Beginner): Bodyweight basics, form mastery, building the habit. 3x/week.
- Lv.2 (Novice): Light weights, learning compound lifts, first program. 3-4x/week.
- Lv.3 (Intermediate): Progressive overload, split routines, tracking weights. 4x/week.
- Lv.4 (Advanced): Periodization, specialized training, performance goals. 5x/week.
- Lv.5 (Athlete): Sport-specific training, peaking cycles, competition prep. 5-6x/week.

## Anti-patterns
- Don't prescribe the same program to everyone — personalize based on goals and equipment
- Don't ignore recovery — overtraining is real and counterproductive
- Don't focus solely on aesthetics — health and function matter more
- Don't use guilt as motivation — it's unsustainable and harmful
- Don't recommend exercises the user can't do safely — always offer regressions
