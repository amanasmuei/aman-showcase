# Savings Goals Guide

## Priority Order

### 1. Emergency Fund (First Priority)
- **Target:** 3-6 months of essential expenses
- **Why:** Protects against job loss, medical emergencies, unexpected repairs
- **How:** Set aside a fixed amount from every paycheck before anything else
- **Milestone path:**
  - RM 500 → covers a minor emergency
  - RM 1,000 → 1 week of expenses
  - RM 3,000 → 1 month buffer
  - RM 10,000 → ~3 month safety net
  - RM 20,000+ → 6 month fortress

### 2. High-Interest Debt (Second Priority)
- Pay off credit card debt before saving for goals (credit card interest > savings returns)
- Snowball method: pay off smallest debt first (motivation wins)
- Avalanche method: pay off highest interest first (math wins)
- Either works — pick what keeps you going

### 3. Specific Goals (Third Priority)
Common goals and realistic timelines:

| Goal | Typical Cost | Save RM 500/month | Save RM 1,000/month |
|------|-------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| New phone | RM 3,000 | 6 months | 3 months |
| Holiday trip | RM 5,000 | 10 months | 5 months |
| Wedding fund | RM 20,000 | 40 months | 20 months |
| Car down payment | RM 10,000 | 20 months | 10 months |
| House down payment (10%) | RM 30,000+ | 60 months | 30 months |

## Making Goals Stick
- Name your goal specifically — "Japan trip March 2027" not "travel fund"
- Set a deadline — open-ended goals never finish
- Automate if possible — transfer on payday, before you can spend it
- Track visually — seeing RM 3,500 / RM 5,000 (70%) is motivating
- Celebrate milestones — 25%, 50%, 75% marks deserve acknowledgment
