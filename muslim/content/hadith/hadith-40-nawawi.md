# Hadith 40 Imam Nawawi
## Al-Arba'un Al-Nawawiyyah — First 10 Hadith (v1)

> These are among the most important hadith in Islam, compiled by Imam Yahya ibn Sharaf al-Nawawi (631–676 AH). All hadith here are from sahih (authentic) collections. This companion shares these as learning and reflection — not as religious rulings. For rulings, consult a qualified scholar.

---

## Hadith 1 — Actions Are by Intentions

**Arabic:**
> إِنَّمَا الْأَعْمَالُ بِالنِّيَّاتِ، وَإِنَّمَا لِكُلِّ امْرِئٍ مَا نَوَى

**Transliteration:**
Innamal a'malu bin niyyat, wa innamal likulli mri'in ma nawa...

**Translation:**
"Actions are [judged] by intentions, and every person will have what they intended. Whoever emigrated for the sake of Allah and His Messenger, their emigration was for Allah and His Messenger. Whoever emigrated for worldly gain or to marry a woman, their emigration was for what they emigrated for."

**Source:** Narrated by Umar ibn al-Khattab (RA) — Sahih Bukhari & Sahih Muslim

**Lesson:**
Niyyah (intention) is the soul of every action. A mundane act like eating becomes ibadah when you intend to nourish your body to better worship Allah. Before any action — ask yourself: why am I doing this? That "why" determines its value before Allah.

## Hadith 2 — Hadith of Jibreel: Islam, Iman, and Ihsan

**Arabic:**
> الْإِسْلَامُ أَنْ تَشْهَدَ أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَأَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ، وَتُقِيمَ الصَّلَاةَ، وَتُؤْتِيَ الزَّكَاةَ، وَتَصُومَ رَمَضَانَ، وَتَحُجَّ الْبَيْتَ إِنِ اسْتَطَعْتَ إِلَيْهِ سَبِيلًا

**Transliteration:**
Al-islamu an tashhada an la ilaha illallah wa anna Muhammadan rasulullah, wa tuqimas salaah, wa tu'tiyaz zakaah, wa tasuma ramadhan, wa tahujjal bayta inistata'ta ilayhi sabila...

**Translation:**
"Islam is to testify that there is no god but Allah and that Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah, to establish prayer, to give zakat, to fast Ramadan, and to perform Hajj if you are able..."

"Iman is to believe in Allah, His angels, His books, His messengers, the Last Day, and to believe in divine decree — both the good and the bad thereof..."

"Ihsan is to worship Allah as though you see Him, and if you cannot see Him, then indeed He sees you..."

**Source:** Narrated by Umar ibn al-Khattab (RA) — Sahih Muslim

**Lesson:**
Islam has three dimensions: external practice (Islam), internal belief (Iman), and excellence of worship (Ihsan). Ihsan — worshipping as if you see Allah — is the highest station. It transforms every prayer from ritual into genuine connection. Strive for Ihsan in even the smallest acts.

## Hadith 3 — The Five Pillars of Islam

**Arabic:**
> بُنِيَ الْإِسْلَامُ عَلَى خَمْسٍ: شَهَادَةِ أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللهُ وَأَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا رَسُولُ اللهِ، وَإِقَامِ الصَّلَاةِ، وَإِيتَاءِ الزَّكَاةِ، وَحَجِّ الْبَيْتِ، وَصَوْمِ رَمَضَانَ

**Transliteration:**
Buniyal islamu 'ala khamsin: shahadati an la ilaha illallahu wa anna Muhammadan rasulullah, wa iqamis salaah, wa ita'iz zakaah, wa hajjil bayt, wa sawmi ramadhan.

**Translation:**
"Islam is built upon five [pillars]: the testimony that there is no god but Allah and that Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah, establishing prayer, giving zakat, performing Hajj to the House, and fasting Ramadan."

**Source:** Narrated by Abdullah ibn Umar (RA) — Sahih Bukhari & Sahih Muslim

**Lesson:**
The five pillars are the structural foundation of Muslim life — not a checklist to complete, but a living architecture that holds your deen together. Each pillar supports the others. Consistent daily prayer (salat) is the one you return to five times every single day — it is the pillar most directly within your daily reach.

## Hadith 4 — Creation in the Womb and Divine Decree

**Arabic:**
> إِنَّ أَحَدَكُمْ يُجْمَعُ خَلْقُهُ فِي بَطْنِ أُمِّهِ أَرْبَعِينَ يَوْمًا نُطْفَةً، ثُمَّ يَكُونُ عَلَقَةً مِثْلَ ذَلِكَ، ثُمَّ يَكُونُ مُضْغَةً مِثْلَ ذَلِكَ، ثُمَّ يُرْسَلُ إِلَيْهِ الْمَلَكُ فَيَنْفُخُ فِيهِ الرُّوحَ

**Transliteration:**
Inna ahadakum yujma'u khalquhu fi batni ummihi arba'ina yawman nutfatan, thumma yakunu 'alaqatan mithla dhalik, thumma yakunu mudhghatan mithla dhalik, thumma yursalu ilayhil malaku fayunfakhu fihir ruh...

**Translation:**
"Each of you is collected in the womb of his mother for forty days as a drop of fluid, then it becomes a clinging form for a similar period, then a lump of flesh for a similar period. Then an angel is sent and breathes the soul into it, and it is commanded regarding four matters: its provision, its lifespan, its deeds, and whether it will be wretched or blessed..."

**Source:** Narrated by Abdullah ibn Mas'ud (RA) — Sahih Bukhari & Sahih Muslim

**Lesson:**
Before you were born, your story was already written by Allah. This is not fatalism — it is liberation. Understanding qadar (divine decree) frees you from anxiety about what you cannot control, while keeping you accountable for your choices. Your deeds were decreed, and you are also responsible for them — both truths exist together in Islam.

## Hadith 5 — Every Innovation in Religion Is Rejected

**Arabic:**
> مَنْ أَحْدَثَ فِي أَمْرِنَا هَذَا مَا لَيْسَ مِنْهُ فَهُوَ رَدٌّ

**Transliteration:**
Man ahdatha fi amrina hadha ma laysa minhu fahuwa radd.

**Translation:**
"Whoever introduces into this affair of ours [i.e., Islam] something that is not part of it, it will be rejected."

In another narration: "Whoever does an action that is not in accordance with our matter, it will be rejected."

**Source:** Narrated by Aisha (RA) — Sahih Muslim (also in Bukhari in slightly different wording)

**Lesson:**
Islam is complete — nothing needs to be added to it. This hadith guards the purity of ibadah: worship Allah only as He and His Prophet SAW taught. It encourages us to learn authentic practices from reliable sources rather than following innovations (bid'ah). When in doubt about a practice, ask: did the Prophet SAW or his companions do this?

## Hadith 6 — Halal Is Clear and Haram Is Clear

**Arabic:**
> إِنَّ الْحَلَالَ بَيِّنٌ وَإِنَّ الْحَرَامَ بَيِّنٌ، وَبَيْنَهُمَا أُمُورٌ مُشْتَبِهَاتٌ لَا يَعْلَمُهُنَّ كَثِيرٌ مِنَ النَّاسِ

**Transliteration:**
Innal halala bayyinun wa innal harama bayyinun, wa baynahuma umurun mushtabihatun la ya'lamuhunna kathirun minan nas...

**Translation:**
"Indeed, the halal is clear and the haram is clear, and between them are matters that are doubtful which many people do not know. Whoever guards against doubtful matters has protected his religion and his honor. Whoever falls into doubtful matters falls into the haram, like a shepherd who grazes [his flock] near a protected area — he is likely to enter it..."

**Source:** Narrated by Nu'man ibn Bashir (RA) — Sahih Bukhari & Sahih Muslim

**Lesson:**
Most of life is clearly halal — enjoy it. A small portion is clearly haram — avoid it. The wisest approach to the grey area in between is caution: when in doubt, step back. This is not about paralysis but about protecting your heart. The Prophet SAW used the beautiful metaphor of the shepherd: don't graze too close to the boundary — you'll end up crossing it.

## Hadith 7 — Religion Is Nasihah (Sincere Advice)

**Arabic:**
> الدِّينُ النَّصِيحَةُ

**Transliteration:**
Ad-dinu an-nasihah.

**Translation:**
"The religion is nasihah (sincerity/counsel)." They said: "To whom?" He said: "To Allah, to His Book, to His Messenger, to the leaders of the Muslims, and to their common folk."

**Source:** Narrated by Tamim ibn Aws al-Dari (RA) — Sahih Muslim

**Lesson:**
Nasihah means sincere advice given out of genuine care — not criticism, not superiority, but love. Islam is not just personal piety; it includes caring enough about your community to speak the truth kindly. The five recipients remind us that sincerity applies to every level: your relationship with Allah, His revelation, the Prophet SAW, those in authority, and every ordinary Muslim you meet.

## Hadith 8 — The Sanctity of a Muslim's Life

**Arabic:**
> أُمِرْتُ أَنْ أُقَاتِلَ النَّاسَ حَتَّى يَشْهَدُوا أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللهُ وَأَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا رَسُولُ اللهِ، وَيُقِيمُوا الصَّلَاةَ، وَيُؤْتُوا الزَّكَاةَ، فَإِذَا فَعَلُوا ذَلِكَ عَصَمُوا مِنِّي دِمَاءَهُمْ وَأَمْوَالَهُمْ إِلَّا بِحَقِّ الْإِسْلَامِ، وَحِسَابُهُمْ عَلَى اللهِ

**Transliteration:**
Umirtu an uqatilan nasa hatta yashhadu an la ilaha illallahu wa anna Muhammadan rasulullah, wa yuqimus salah, wa yu'tuz zakah, fa idha fa'alu dhalika 'asamu minni dima'ahum wa amwalahum illa bihaqqi islam, wa hisabuhum 'alallah.

**Translation:**
"I have been commanded to fight the people until they testify that there is no god but Allah and that Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah, and establish prayer, and give zakat. If they do that, their blood and wealth are protected from me except by the right of Islam, and their reckoning is with Allah."

**Source:** Narrated by Abdullah ibn Umar (RA) — Sahih Bukhari & Sahih Muslim

**Lesson:**
This hadith establishes the profound sanctity of life in Islam. Once someone accepts Islam and fulfills its outward obligations, their life, wealth, and honor are inviolable. Imam Nawawi included this to emphasize that Islam protects life, not endangers it. The inner state of the heart belongs to Allah alone — no human can judge what lies within another.

## Hadith 9 — Leave What Does Not Concern You

**Arabic:**
> مِنْ حُسْنِ إِسْلَامِ الْمَرْءِ تَرْكُهُ مَا لَا يَعْنِيهِ

**Transliteration:**
Min husni islami'l mar'i tarkuhu ma la ya'nih.

**Translation:**
"Part of the perfection of a person's Islam is leaving what does not concern him."

**Source:** Narrated by Abu Hurairah (RA) — narrated by al-Tirmidhi and others; also reported from various companions; Ibn Majah. Graded hasan by al-Nawawi and others.

**Lesson:**
This elegant hadith covers so much: avoid gossip, don't interfere in others' affairs, don't follow every controversy. A mature Muslim focuses on what truly matters — their own ibadah, their family, their work, their community. In the age of social media, this hadith is more relevant than ever. Ask before engaging: does this concern me? If not — leave it.

## Hadith 10 — Allah Accepts Only What Is Pure

**Arabic:**
> إِنَّ اللهَ طَيِّبٌ لَا يَقْبَلُ إِلَّا طَيِّبًا، وَإِنَّ اللهَ أَمَرَ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ بِمَا أَمَرَ بِهِ الْمُرْسَلِينَ

**Transliteration:**
Innallaha tayyibun la yaqbalu illa tayyiban, wa innallaha amara'l mu'minina bima amara bihi'l mursalin...

**Translation:**
"Indeed Allah is pure and He does not accept except what is pure. And indeed Allah has commanded the believers with what He commanded the Messengers. He said: 'O Messengers, eat of the pure things and act righteously.' And He said: 'O you who believe, eat of the pure things We have provided for you.' Then he [the Prophet SAW] mentioned a man who travels far, disheveled and dusty, raising his hands to the sky: 'O Lord! O Lord!' — but his food is haram, his drink is haram, his clothing is haram, he was nourished by haram — so how can his du'a be answered?"

**Source:** Narrated by Abu Hurairah (RA) — Sahih Muslim

**Lesson:**
Purity (tayyib) is a condition for acceptance — in worship, in food, in earnings. This hadith powerfully illustrates that du'a is connected to how we live: the man in the story has all the outward signs of sincerity (traveling, disheveled, hands raised) but his livelihood is haram. This is a reminder to check our sources of income, food, and wealth — not out of fear, but because we want our du'a to reach Allah.
