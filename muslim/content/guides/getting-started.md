# Getting Started with Aman Muslim

## Setup in 3 Minutes

Aman Muslim works best when it knows a few things about you. Here's how to get started.

---

### Step 1: Tell Aman Your Location

Prayer times and qiblat direction depend on where you are. Just say:

> "I'm in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia"
> "My city is Johor Bahru"
> "I live in London, UK"

Aman will remember this and use it for all future prayer time and qiblat calculations. If you travel, just update it: "I'm in Istanbul this week."

---

### Step 2: Choose Your Language

Aman speaks Malay and English. You can say:

> "Saya nak guna Bahasa Melayu"
> "English please"
> "Both — switch based on topic"

The default is bilingual: Islamic terms in Arabic/Malay, explanations in your preferred language.

---

### Step 3: Choose What You Want

Tell Aman what you're looking for. Pick one or all:

| Option | What It Gives You |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Prayer reminders** | Daily prayer times for your city, streak tracking |
| **Daily hadith** | One hadith from Hadith 40 Nawawi per day with explanation |
| **Dhikr guide** | Morning/evening adhkar and after-solat dhikr with Arabic text |
| **Du'a library** | Authentic du'a for every situation with transliteration |
| **Ibadah tracker** | Weekly prayer tracking, hadith progress, streak highlights |
| **All of the above** | Full companion mode — recommended for daily use |

Just say: "I want everything" or "Just daily hadith and prayer times for now."

---

## Tips for Getting the Most Out of Aman Muslim

- **Start your day with Aman** — say "pagi" or "good morning" to get your daily check-in
- **Confirm your prayers** — say "dah solat Zohor" to track your streak
- **Ask for any du'a** — "du'a before sleeping", "du'a for anxiety", "du'a for parents"
- **Check your progress** — say "progress" to see your weekly ibadah summary
- **Questions are welcome** — ask "what is istighfar?" or "how many rakaat is Asar?"

---

## What Aman Muslim Is (and Isn't)

**Aman Muslim IS:**
- A daily companion for ibadah practice
- A guide to authentic du'a, dhikr, and hadith
- A gentle reminder and tracker for your spiritual habits
- A knowledgeable friend who cites sources

**Aman Muslim IS NOT:**
- A mufti or scholar — it cannot give fatwa
- A replacement for your ustaz, imam, or local religious authority
- An interpreter of Quran (that's Aman Quran's role)
- A source of religious rulings on specific situations

For any specific religious ruling or fatwa, always consult your local ustaz, imam, or the relevant Islamic authority (JAKIM, MUFTI, etc.).
