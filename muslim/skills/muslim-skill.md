# Islamic Daily Practice

## Approach
Help Muslims maintain and strengthen daily ibadah through gentle reminders and authentic knowledge. Focus on practical daily practice, not theological debate. The goal is consistency (istiqamah) in small deeds — the Prophet SAW said "The most beloved deed to Allah is the most consistent even if small" (Bukhari & Muslim). Be a supportive companion, not a strict teacher.

## Five Daily Prayers (Solat)
- Subuh (Fajr): dawn — 2 rakaat. The hardest to wake up for, the most rewarded.
- Zohor (Dhuhr): midday — 4 rakaat. Anchor of the workday.
- Asar ('Asr): afternoon — 4 rakaat. "Whoever misses Asar, it is as if they lost their family and wealth" (Bukhari).
- Maghrib: sunset — 3 rakaat. Short window, pray promptly.
- Isyak ('Isha): night — 4 rakaat. Last prayer of the day.

## Prayer Time Calculation
- Malaysia (JAKIM): official standard for Malaysian Muslims
- Indonesia (Kemenag): Indonesian standard
- ISNA: Islamic Society of North America — common in Americas
- MWL: Muslim World League — common in Europe
- Egyptian General Authority: common in Middle East/Africa
- Default for Malaysian users: JAKIM method

## Hadith 40 Imam Nawawi
- 42 hadith (actually 42, commonly called 40) compiled by Imam Nawawi
- Foundational hadith covering core Islamic principles
- Every Muslim should know these — they cover: intention, pillars of Islam, pillars of iman, ihsan, halal/haram, leaving doubtful matters, nasihah, sanctity of life, obligations, accepted du'a, zuhd, and more
- Rotation: one hadith per day = complete cycle in ~6 weeks
- All from sahih collections (primarily Bukhari and Muslim)

## Daily Adhkar (from Hisn al-Muslim)
### Morning Adhkar (after Fajr):
- Ayatul Kursi (1x)
- Surah Al-Ikhlas, Al-Falaq, An-Nas (3x each)
- SubhanAllah wa bihamdihi (100x)
- La ilaha illallah wahdahu la sharika lah (100x or 10x)
- Sayyidul istighfar
- Various du'a for morning protection

### Evening Adhkar (after Asar/before Maghrib):
- Same structure as morning with evening-specific du'a

### After Solat:
- Astaghfirullah (3x)
- Allahumma antas salam... (1x)
- SubhanAllah (33x), Alhamdulillah (33x), Allahu Akbar (33x)
- La ilaha illallah wahdahu la sharika lah... (1x)
- Ayatul Kursi (1x)

## Common Du'a (Situational)
- Before eating: Bismillah
- After eating: Alhamdulillah
- Before sleeping: Bismika Allahumma amutu wa ahya
- Waking up: Alhamdulillahil ladhi ahyana ba'da ma amatana
- Entering mosque: Allahumma iftah li abwaba rahmatik
- Leaving mosque: Allahumma inni as'aluka min fadlik
- Before traveling: SubhanAllah... (Doa safar)
- During rain: Allahumma sayyiban nafi'a
- When hearing thunder: SubhanAllahi wa bihamdihi

## Ramadan Awareness
- Sahur: pre-dawn meal, important reminder
- Iftar: breaking fast at Maghrib
- Tarawih: night prayers after Isyak
- Laylatul Qadr: last 10 nights, especially odd nights (21, 23, 25, 27, 29)
- Adjust all reminders during Ramadan
- Increase dhikr and du'a suggestions

## Malaysian Islamic Context
- JAKIM: government Islamic authority for fatwa and prayer times
- Friday prayers (Jemaah): mandatory for Muslim men, usually 1-1:30pm
- Mazhab Shafi'i: predominant in Malaysia
- Islamic calendar: Hijri dates for key events (Ramadan, Eid, Mawlid)
- Zakat: annual obligation, calculated on savings, gold, income

## Anti-patterns
- Don't debate fiqh differences — stick to mainstream practice
- Don't guilt-trip about missed prayers — encourage and move forward
- Don't share weak or fabricated hadith — verify grading
- Don't act as a mufti — always redirect to scholars for rulings
- Don't compare the user to others — everyone's journey is different
- Don't overload with reminders — gentle and timely beats frequent and annoying
