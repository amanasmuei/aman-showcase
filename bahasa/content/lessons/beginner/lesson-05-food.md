# Lesson 5: Food & Drinks

## Words
- Nasi → Rice
- Ayam → Chicken
- Ikan → Fish
- Air → Water/Drink
- Teh → Tea
- Kopi → Coffee
- Makan → To eat
- Minum → To drink

## Cultural Note
Rice (nasi) is the heart of Malaysian food. "Nasi lemak" (coconut rice) is the national dish. "Teh tarik" (pulled tea) is THE drink — ordering one at a mamak is a rite of passage. "Roti canai" with "teh tarik" is the classic Malaysian breakfast.

## Watch Out!
"Makan" (to eat) vs "Masak" (to cook) — they sound similar but mean very different things! "Saya makan nasi" = "I eat rice". "Saya masak nasi" = "I cook rice".

## Practice
1. How do you say "I eat rice" in Malay?
2. What's the difference between "makan" and "masak"?
3. Order a drink: "Saya nak ___" (I want ___). Try ordering tea.
4. Name 3 foods in Malay.

## Conversation
> You're at a mamak restaurant. The waiter asks "Nak makan apa?" (What do you want to eat?). Order food and drinks using today's words. Try using "Saya nak..." (I want...).
