# Lesson 8: At the Market

## Words
- Berapa? → How much?
- Ringgit → Malaysian currency (RM)
- Mahal → Expensive
- Murah → Cheap
- Beli → Buy
- Jual → Sell
- Satu kilo → One kilogram
- Boleh kurang? → Can you reduce (the price)?

## Cultural Note
Bargaining is common at Malaysian markets (pasar) and night markets (pasar malam). "Boleh kurang?" is your magic phrase! But don't bargain at fixed-price shops or malls.

## Watch Out!
"Beli" (buy) vs "Jual" (sell) — don't mix them up! "Saya nak beli" = "I want to buy". "Dia jual ikan" = "He/she sells fish".

## Practice
1. How do you ask "How much?" in Malay?
2. Something costs RM 50. You think it's expensive. What do you say?
3. How do you ask for a discount?
4. Say: "I want to buy two kilograms of chicken" — combine numbers + food + market words!

## Conversation
> You're at a pasar malam (night market). A vendor is selling fruit. Ask the price, try to bargain, and buy something. Use numbers from Lesson 3 and food words from Lesson 5.
