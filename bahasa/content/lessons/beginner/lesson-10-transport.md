# Lesson 10: Transport

## Words
- Kereta → Car
- Bas → Bus
- Teksi → Taxi
- Kereta api → Train (literally "fire car"!)
- Naik → To ride/take (transport)
- Turun → To get off/alight
- Berhenti → To stop
- Stesen → Station

## Fun Fact
"Kereta api" literally means "fire car" — from the old steam train days! Malaysians also say "LRT" and "MRT" for the city rail systems, just like in English.

## Pattern
"Naik" + transport = "take [transport]"
- "Saya naik bas" = "I take the bus"
- "Saya naik teksi" = "I take a taxi"
- "Turun di stesen" = "Get off at the station"

## Practice
1. How do you say "I take the train" in Malay?
2. What does "turun" mean? Use it in a sentence.
3. Tell the taxi driver: "Stop here please" → "Berhenti di sini"
4. Combine with directions: "Take the bus, get off at the station, turn left"

## Conversation
> You need to get from your hotel to KLCC (the famous twin towers). Ask a local how to get there. They explain which transport to take, where to get off, and which direction to walk. Use transport, directions, and market vocabulary from previous lessons.
