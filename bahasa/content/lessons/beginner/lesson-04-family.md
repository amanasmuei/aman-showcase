# Lesson 4: Family

## Words
- Ibu → Mother
- Ayah → Father
- Adik → Younger sibling
- Abang → Older brother
- Kakak → Older sister
- Anak → Child/Son/Daughter
- Keluarga → Family
- Berapa orang? → How many people?

## Cultural Note
Malaysians use family terms for strangers too! Call an older man "abang" or "pakcik" (uncle), an older woman "kakak" or "makcik" (auntie). It's respectful and friendly. A shopkeeper might call you "adik" (younger sibling) if you're younger than them.

## Grammar Note
"Saya ada" = "I have". "Saya ada dua adik" = "I have two younger siblings". Numbers come before the noun, just like English!

## Practice
1. How do you say "mother" and "father" in Malay?
2. What's the difference between "abang" and "adik"?
3. Say "I have one older sister" in Malay.
4. How many people are in your family? Answer: "Keluarga saya ada ___ orang."
