# Medication Tips

## General Tips
- Take medication at the same time every day — consistency helps it work
- Use meals as anchors: "after breakfast", "with lunch", "before bed"
- Keep medications in a visible place — next to the kettle, by the TV remote
- Don't stop taking medication just because you feel better — consult your doctor first
- If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember — unless it's almost time for the next dose

## Common Malaysian Medications

### Ubat Darah Tinggi (Blood Pressure)
- Usually taken: morning
- With/without food: either is fine for most
- Common names: Amlodipine, Losartan, Perindopril
- Important: don't skip — blood pressure rises silently

### Ubat Kencing Manis (Diabetes)
- Usually taken: with meals (Metformin), before meals (Gliclazide)
- Important: take WITH food to avoid stomach upset (Metformin)
- Monitor: blood sugar levels as directed by doctor
- Ramadan: consult doctor about timing changes during fasting

### Ubat Kolesterol (Cholesterol)
- Usually taken: at night (Simvastatin, Atorvastatin)
- Why night: the body makes more cholesterol while sleeping
- With/without food: either is fine
- Don't take with grapefruit juice

### Vitamin D
- Usually taken: with food (fat helps absorption)
- Common dose: 1000 IU daily
- Many Malaysians are deficient despite the sun!

## Ramadan Medication Guide
- **Consult your doctor** before changing any medication timing
- Most once-daily medications: take at Sahur (pre-dawn) or Iftar (breaking fast)
- Twice-daily: one at Sahur, one at Iftar
- Insulin: requires doctor adjustment for fasting
- **Never adjust doses yourself** — always ask your doctor

## When to See a Doctor
- Feeling dizzy or unwell after taking medication
- New side effects (rash, swelling, nausea)
- Want to stop or change a medication
- Adding a new supplement alongside medication
- Difficulty swallowing tablets
- Medication running out — get refill before it's finished!

## Disclaimer
This guide is for general awareness only. Always follow your doctor's specific instructions for your medications. Aman Rutin is a reminder tool, not a medical advisor.
