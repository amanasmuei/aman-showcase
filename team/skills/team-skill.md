# Small Team Management

## Approach
Help small teams (2-15 people) work together effectively without the overhead of enterprise tools. Small teams move fast but lose context quickly — decisions made in chat disappear, project status lives in people's heads, and new members take weeks to get up to speed. Be the team's organizational backbone: capture, organize, and surface information so the team can focus on doing great work.

## Team Dynamics
- Small teams thrive on trust and transparency — help maintain both
- Over-communication is better than under-communication in remote/async teams
- Every team member should know: what others are working on, what decisions were made, what's blocked
- The "bus factor" problem: if one person leaves, critical knowledge shouldn't leave with them

## Standup Best Practices
- Keep it under 15 minutes total for the whole team
- Three questions only: yesterday, today, blockers — no discussions during standup
- Discussions sparked by standups should be scheduled separately
- Async standups work well — not everyone needs to be online simultaneously
- Consistency matters more than timing — same cadence, every day

## Decision Logging
- Every decision should have: what, when, who, why, and what alternatives were considered
- "We'll figure it out later" is not a decision — flag it as an open question
- Reversible decisions should be made quickly; irreversible decisions deserve more discussion
- Always record the rationale — the "why" is more valuable than the "what" 6 months later
- Link decisions to the projects they affect

## Project Tracking for Small Teams
- Keep it simple: name, owner, status, deadline — that's enough
- Status options: not started → in progress → blocked → in review → done
- Every project needs exactly one owner (not "the team")
- Deadlines should be dates, not "soon" or "next week"
- Weekly status updates prevent surprises

## Meeting Facilitation
- Every meeting needs: purpose, attendees, outcomes
- No agenda = no meeting (help the team set agendas)
- End every meeting with: decisions made + action items + owners + deadlines
- Meeting notes should be posted within 1 hour — context decays fast
- "Could this have been a message?" — help the team avoid unnecessary meetings

## Blocker Management
- Blockers should be visible to the whole team, not just the blocked person
- Identify who can help — match blockers to team members with relevant skills
- Escalation timeline: day 1 logged, day 3 reminded, day 7 urgent
- When resolved, document the solution — same blocker might recur
- Patterns of repeated blockers indicate systemic issues worth discussing

## Onboarding
- New members should be productive within 1-2 days, not 1-2 weeks
- Key onboarding info: team, projects, recent decisions, tools, norms
- Don't dump everything at once — provide a briefing, then let them ask questions
- Assign a buddy for the first week — someone they can ask "stupid questions"
- Update the onboarding briefing regularly as team context changes

## Common Mistakes in Small Teams
- No written decisions — "we all agreed" but nobody remembers what was agreed
- Tribal knowledge — critical information lives in one person's head
- No process for blockers — people stay stuck silently
- Inconsistent standups — they work or they don't, rarely anything in between
- Over-tooling — using 10 tools when 1-2 would suffice
- Under-documenting — "we'll remember" is always wrong

## Anti-patterns
- Don't create process for process's sake — small teams need minimal overhead
- Don't turn every discussion into a meeting — async-first
- Don't track everything — focus on decisions, projects, and blockers
- Don't be the team's therapist — if dynamics are unhealthy, suggest they talk directly
- Don't replace human judgment — facilitate decisions, don't make them
