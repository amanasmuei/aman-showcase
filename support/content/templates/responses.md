# Aman Support — Response Templates

These templates are starting points. Personalize every response with the customer's name and specific details. A template that sounds like a template defeats the purpose.

---

## Greetings

### New customer
```
Hi [name]! Welcome — glad you reached out. How can I help you today?
```

```
Hello [name]! Thanks for getting in touch. What can I do for you?
```

### Returning customer (with history)
```
Hey [name]! Good to hear from you again. Last time you were in touch about [previous issue] — I hope that's all sorted. What can I help you with today?
```

```
Welcome back, [name]! Hope everything's been going well. What brings you in today?
```

### Returning customer (neutral, no specific history to reference)
```
Hi [name], great to hear from you again! What can I help you with?
```

## Acknowledgment

### Standard acknowledgment
```
I understand your concern about [issue]. Let me look into this for you right now.
```

### Frustrated customer acknowledgment
```
I'm really sorry to hear you're dealing with this, [name]. That sounds genuinely frustrating, and I want to make sure we get it sorted for you. Give me a moment to review your situation.
```

### Complaint acknowledgment
```
Thank you for bringing this to my attention, [name]. I take this seriously and I want to understand exactly what happened so we can make it right. Can you walk me through what occurred?
```

### Acknowledgment + ticket number
```
I've logged your issue under ticket [TKT-XXXX]. You can reference this number if you follow up. Now let me work on getting this resolved.
```

## FAQ Answer Format

```
Great question! Here's the answer:

[Direct answer in 1–3 sentences.]

[Optional: one additional helpful detail or caveat.]

Does that answer your question, or is there anything else I can help with?
```

Example:
```
Great question! Here's the answer:

We ship all orders within 2–3 business days. You'll receive a tracking link via email once your order is on its way.

Note that weekends and public holidays are not counted as business days.

Does that answer your question, or is there anything else I can help with?
```

## Escalation Messages

### Escalating to owner (customer-facing)
```
I want to make sure this gets the right attention, [name]. I'm connecting you with [owner name] who can help you directly with this.

Your ticket number is [TKT-XXXX] — please keep this for your reference. [Owner name] will follow up with you shortly.
```

### Escalating due to refund/compensation
```
Thank you for your patience, [name]. This involves a decision that I want to make sure is handled properly, so I'm passing this to [owner name] to review personally.

Ticket [TKT-XXXX] has been flagged as a priority. You'll hear back soon.
```

### Escalating at customer's request
```
Of course — I'll connect you with a real person right away, [name].

I've shared your full conversation and issue details with [owner name] so you won't have to repeat yourself. Ticket [TKT-XXXX] is on their radar now.
```

## Resolution Confirmation

### After resolving an issue
```
I'm glad we could sort that out! Just to confirm — [brief restatement of what was resolved]. Is there anything else I can help you with?
```

### After a longer or complex resolution
```
Thank you for your patience while we worked through this, [name]. To recap what we've done: [summary of resolution]. Ticket [TKT-XXXX] is now marked as resolved.

If the issue comes up again or you have any follow-up questions, just message here and I'll have your history on hand.
```

## Follow-Up Check

### Standard follow-up (unresolved ticket)
```
Hi [name], just checking in on your ticket [TKT-XXXX] regarding [issue summary]. Has this been resolved for you, or is it still affecting you?

Let me know and we'll get right on it.
```

### Follow-up after escalation
```
Hi [name], I wanted to follow up on the issue you raised about [issue summary]. Our team is still working on it — I just wanted to make sure you know it hasn't been forgotten.

Is there any update on your end, or anything new I should pass along?
```

## Apology Templates

### Sincere apology (not corporate)
```
I'm really sorry about this, [name]. This isn't the experience we want you to have, and I genuinely apologize for the inconvenience. Here's what I'm going to do to fix it: [action].
```

### Apology for delay
```
I apologize for how long this has taken, [name]. You deserved a faster resolution and I'm sorry we haven't delivered that. Let me prioritize this right now.
```

### Apology for error
```
You're right, and I'm sorry — [acknowledge specific error]. That shouldn't have happened. I'm fixing it now and making sure it doesn't happen again.
```

Note: Avoid "We apologize for any inconvenience caused." It is hollow and customers know it. Be specific about what went wrong and what you are doing about it.

## Thank You and Closing

### After resolution + positive feedback
```
That's great to hear! So glad we could help, [name]. Don't hesitate to reach out if you ever need anything — we're always here.
```

### After resolution + neutral close
```
Glad that's sorted! Take care, [name], and feel free to get in touch any time.
```

### After resolution + low satisfaction score
```
Thank you for the honest feedback, [name] — it helps us improve. I'm sorry we didn't fully meet your expectations this time. If there's anything specific we could have done better, I'd genuinely like to know.
```

### Closing with satisfaction survey prompt
```
Before you go — how would you rate your experience today? Just reply with a number from 1 (not great) to 5 (excellent). It only takes a second and it means a lot to us.
```
