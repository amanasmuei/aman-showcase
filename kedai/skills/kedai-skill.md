# Small Restaurant Management

## Approach
Help small food business owners (warung, mamak, kedai makan, food stalls) manage their daily operations through simple conversation. These owners are usually cooking, serving, and managing simultaneously — they need an assistant that's as fast as their kitchen. Focus on order accuracy, customer memory, and daily sales tracking.

## Malaysian Food Business Context
- Mamak restaurants: 24-hour, roti canai, nasi kandar, teh tarik — high volume, fast turnover
- Warung/kedai makan: rice-based meals, mixed rice (nasi campur), home-style cooking
- Food stalls (gerai): night market, food court, specialized items
- Typical margins: 30-50% on food, 60-80% on drinks
- Peak hours: breakfast (7-9am), lunch (12-2pm), dinner (6-9pm), supper (10pm-1am for mamak)

## Order Management
- Speed is everything — confirm in under 5 seconds
- Always repeat the order back — mistakes cost money and reputation
- Handle modifications naturally: "kurang manis" (less sweet), "extra sambal", "no onion"
- Dine-in vs takeaway vs delivery affects packaging and sometimes price
- Numbered orders prevent confusion during busy periods

## Customer Relationship
- Regular customers are the backbone of small food businesses — up to 70% of revenue
- Remembering "the usual" makes customers feel valued and speeds up service
- Allergies and dietary requirements are critical — one mistake can be dangerous
- Track "haven't seen in a while" — a regular who stops coming is worth reaching out to
- Birthday/special occasion notes are a nice touch for VIP regulars

## Menu Optimization
- Track what sells and what doesn't — data beats intuition
- Daily specials can test new items with low risk
- Price sensitivity: RM 1 increase on a popular item is felt immediately
- Combo meals increase average order value: "Nasi Lemak + Teh Tarik = RM 10" (saves RM 1.50)
- Sold-out items should be communicated immediately — don't let customers order unavailable food

## Common Malay Food Terms
- Nasi: rice (nasi lemak, nasi goreng, nasi kandar, nasi campur)
- Mee: noodles (mee goreng, mee sup, mee rebus)
- Roti: bread (roti canai, roti telur, roti sardin)
- Teh: tea (teh tarik, teh ais, teh O)
- Kopi: coffee (kopi O, kopi ais, kopi tarik)
- Kurang manis: less sweet
- Tambah: add more
- Bungkus: takeaway (wrapped)
- Makan sini: dine in (eat here)

## Daily Operations
- Morning: check inventory, prepare daily specials, review yesterday's numbers
- During service: log orders, manage sold-out items, handle rush periods
- End of day: daily summary, reconcile orders, plan for tomorrow
- Weekly: review trends, adjust menu, restock planning

## Financial Tracking
- Track revenue per day, not just per order
- Average order value: RM 8-15 for mamak, RM 10-20 for kedai makan
- Food cost ratio: aim for 30-35% (food cost / selling price)
- Popular items with high margins are your best friends
- Low-selling items that require fresh ingredients should be reconsidered

## Common Challenges
- Rush hour chaos: orders pile up, kitchen falls behind — numbered orders and quick confirmations help
- Inventory waste: cooking too much → throwing away food → lost money
- Staff communication: owner takes order, kitchen doesn't get it right — written confirmation helps
- Pricing: charging too little because "it's a small place" — know your costs
- No data: most small restaurants don't track what sells — this is the #1 thing to fix
