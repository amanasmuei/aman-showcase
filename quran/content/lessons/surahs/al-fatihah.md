# Surah Al-Fatihah — The Opening

The most recited surah in the world — every Muslim recites it at least 17 times daily in prayer. Understanding its 7 verses transforms your salah.

## Verse 1
بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ

*Bismillah ir-Rahman ir-Raheem*
In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.

**Key Words:**
- بِسْمِ (bismi) → in the name of — from root س-م-و (to be elevated/named)
- اللَّهِ (Allah) → God
- الرَّحْمَٰنِ (ar-Rahman) → The Most Gracious — from root ر-ح-م (mercy). Intensive form: overwhelmingly merciful
- الرَّحِيمِ (ar-Raheem) → The Most Merciful — same root ر-ح-م. Consistent, ongoing mercy

**Root Spotlight:** ر-ح-م (r-h-m) = mercy. Both Rahman and Raheem come from this root. Rahman = vast, overwhelming mercy. Raheem = continuous, personal mercy.

## Verse 2
الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ

*Al-hamdu lillahi Rabbil 'aalameen*
All praise is due to Allah, Lord of the worlds.

**Key Words:**
- الْحَمْدُ (al-hamd) → praise/gratitude — root ح-م-د (to praise)
- لِلَّهِ (lillahi) → to/for Allah
- رَبِّ (Rabb) → Lord/Sustainer — root ر-ب-ب (to nurture, sustain)
- الْعَالَمِينَ (al-'aalameen) → the worlds/all creation — root ع-ل-م (to know)

## Verse 3
الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ

*Ar-Rahman ir-Raheem*
The Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.

**Key Words:** Same as Verse 1 — reinforcement of Allah's mercy. Notice the repetition: the Quran emphasizes mercy above all other attributes.

## Verse 4
مَالِكِ يَوْمِ الدِّينِ

*Maliki yawmid-deen*
Master of the Day of Judgment.

**Key Words:**
- مَالِكِ (Maliki) → Master/Owner — root م-ل-ك (to own, possess)
- يَوْمِ (yawmi) → day — one of the most common Quran words (~475 times)
- الدِّينِ (ad-deen) → the judgment/the religion — root د-ي-ن (to judge, owe)

## Verse 5
إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ

*Iyyaka na'budu wa iyyaka nasta'een*
You alone we worship, and You alone we ask for help.

**Key Words:**
- إِيَّاكَ (iyyaka) → You alone (emphatic)
- نَعْبُدُ (na'budu) → we worship — root ع-ب-د (to worship/serve)
- نَسْتَعِينُ (nasta'een) → we seek help — root ع-و-ن (to help). Form X: seeking/requesting

## Verse 6
اهْدِنَا الصِّرَاطَ الْمُسْتَقِيمَ

*Ihdinas-siratal-mustaqeem*
Guide us to the straight path.

**Key Words:**
- اهْدِنَا (ihdina) → guide us — root ه-د-ي (to guide). Huda (guidance) comes from this root
- الصِّرَاطَ (as-sirat) → the path
- الْمُسْتَقِيمَ (al-mustaqeem) → the straight/upright — root ق-و-م (to stand firm)

## Verse 7
صِرَاطَ الَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ غَيْرِ الْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا الضَّالِّينَ

*Siratal-ladhina an'amta 'alayhim ghayril-maghdubi 'alayhim walad-dalleen*
The path of those You have blessed, not of those who earned anger, nor of those who went astray.

**Key Words:**
- الَّذِينَ (alladhina) → those who — extremely common connector (~1,464 times)
- أَنْعَمْتَ (an'amta) → You blessed — root ن-ع-م (blessing, comfort)
- الْمَغْضُوبِ (al-maghdubi) → those who earned anger — root غ-ض-ب (anger)
- الضَّالِّينَ (ad-dalleen) → those who went astray — root ض-ل-ل (to stray)

## Summary
Al-Fatihah contains approximately 25 unique words. After studying this surah, you now know words that appear thousands of times across the Quran: Allah, Rabb, Rahman, Raheem, yawm, sirat, huda.
