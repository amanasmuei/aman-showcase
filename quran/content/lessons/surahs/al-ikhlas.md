# Surah Al-Ikhlas — The Sincerity

One of the shortest surahs yet one of the most profound — the Prophet (peace be upon him) said it equals one-third of the Quran. It is a complete declaration of Tawhid (the Oneness of Allah) in 4 verses.

## Verse 1
قُلْ هُوَ اللَّهُ أَحَدٌ

*Qul huwa Allahu Ahad*
Say: He is Allah, [who is] One.

**Key Words:**
- قُلْ (qul) → say — root ق-و-ل (to say/speak). Command form: "say!" This word opens three of the last four surahs, making it one of the most important command words in the Quran
- هُوَ (huwa) → He — third-person singular pronoun. Used to indicate the divine subject
- اللَّهُ (Allah) → God — the proper name of the Creator
- أَحَدٌ (Ahad) → One — root أ-ح-د (oneness, uniqueness). This is absolute, exclusive Oneness — not merely "one among many" but uniquely singular in all existence

**Root Spotlight:** أ-ح-د (a-h-d) = oneness. Ahad implies a uniqueness that cannot be shared or divided. Compare with wahid (also "one") — Ahad denotes absolute singularity.

## Verse 2
اللَّهُ الصَّمَدُ

*Allahu as-Samad*
Allah, the Eternal Refuge.

**Key Words:**
- الصَّمَدُ (as-Samad) → the Eternal Refuge/Self-Sufficient — root ص-م-د (to be solid, to be the one aimed at in need). Samad is a unique Quranic word: it means the one to whom all creation turns in need, who Himself needs nothing. Some scholars say this word is untranslatable in its full depth

**Root Spotlight:** ص-م-د (s-m-d) = to be solid, impenetrable, and the ultimate source. As-Samad means Allah is entirely self-sufficient and everything depends on Him.

## Verse 3
لَمْ يَلِدْ وَلَمْ يُولَدْ

*Lam yalid wa lam yulad*
He begets not, nor was He begotten.

**Key Words:**
- لَمْ (lam) → did not/has not — negation particle used with the past-tense verb form (jussive)
- يَلِدْ (yalid) → He begets/He fathers — root و-ل-د (to give birth, to beget). Active voice: Allah does not father or produce offspring
- وَ (wa) → and — simple conjunction
- يُولَدْ (yulad) → He is begotten/born — same root و-ل-د but passive voice. Allah was not brought into existence by another

**Root Spotlight:** و-ل-د (w-l-d) = birth, offspring. Walad = child. Waalid = parent. This verse directly refutes those who claim Allah has a son or was Himself born — two central theological corrections.

## Verse 4
وَلَمْ يَكُن لَّهُ كُفُوًا أَحَدٌ

*Wa lam yakun lahu kufuwan ahad*
And there is none comparable to Him.

**Key Words:**
- يَكُن (yakun) → is/exists — root ك-و-ن (to be, to exist). Jussive form after لَمْ
- لَّهُ (lahu) → for Him/to Him — prepositional phrase
- كُفُوًا (kufuwan) → equivalent/comparable — root ك-ف-أ (to be equal, to match). A kuf' is an equal match. Nothing in creation is an equal match for Allah
- أَحَدٌ (ahad) → anyone — same root as verse 1. Here it means "not a single one" of all creation can compare to Allah

**Root Spotlight:** ك-ف-أ (k-f-') = equivalence, matching. Kufuwan means a perfect counterpart. The verse asserts no such counterpart exists for Allah anywhere in creation.

## Summary
Al-Ikhlas packs an entire theology into 15 words. The four verses establish: (1) Allah is uniquely One, (2) Allah is the eternal source all creation depends on, (3) Allah has no parentage or offspring, (4) nothing equals or resembles Allah. Knowing قُلْ, هُوَ, أَحَدٌ, and الصَّمَدُ gives you the heart of Islamic theology.
