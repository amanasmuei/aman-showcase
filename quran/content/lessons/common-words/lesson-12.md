# Lesson 12: Provision, Guidance, and Divine Perception

## Words

- رِزْق (rizq) → provision / sustenance / livelihood — appears ~123 times (root occurrences)
  Ayah: "وَفِي السَّمَاءِ رِزْقُكُمْ وَمَا تُوعَدُونَ" — "And in the heaven is your provision and whatever you are promised." (51:22)

- هُدَى (huda) → guidance — appears ~188 times
  Ayah: "إِنَّ هَٰذَا الْقُرْآنَ يَهْدِي لِلَّتِي هِيَ أَقْوَمُ" — "Indeed, this Quran guides to that which is most suitable." (17:9)

- ضَلَال (dalal) → misguidance / going astray / error — appears ~191 times (root occurrences)
  Ayah: "وَمَن يَكْفُرْ بِاللَّهِ وَمَلَائِكَتِهِ وَكُتُبِهِ وَرُسُلِهِ فَقَدْ ضَلَّ ضَلَالًا بَعِيدًا" — "Whoever disbelieves in Allah, His angels, His books, and His messengers has gone far astray." (4:136)

- بَصِير (basir) → seeing / All-Seeing / perceptive — appears ~163 times
  Ayah: "وَهُوَ السَّمِيعُ الْبَصِيرُ" — "And He is the All-Hearing, the All-Seeing." (42:11)

- سَمِيع (sami') → hearing / All-Hearing — appears ~185 times
  Ayah: "إِنَّ اللَّهَ كَانَ سَمِيعًا بَصِيرًا" — "Indeed, Allah is ever Hearing and Seeing." (4:58)

## Root Connection

- **هُدَى** (root: ه-د-ي, h-d-y) → هَدَى (he guided), يَهْدِي (He guides), مُهْتَدٍ (one who is guided), هَادٍ (guide). الْهُدَى is one of the Quran's self-descriptions (2:2 from Lesson 6). The opposite is **ضَلَال** (root: ض-ل-ل, d-l-l) → ضَلَّ (he went astray), الضَّالِّينَ (those who go astray — the final word of Al-Fatihah). These two roots form the central moral axis of the Quran.
- **رِزْق** (root: ر-ز-ق, r-z-q) → رَزَقَ (He provided), الرَّزَّاق (the Great Provider — a divine name). Provision in the Quran is always from God — even human effort is made possible by divine supply.
- **بَصِير** and **سَمِيع** (roots: ب-ص-ر and س-م-ع) are almost always paired in the Quran as divine names. بَصَر (sight/insight), سَمَعَ (he heard), سَمْع (hearing). Together they signify God's total awareness — nothing escapes His sight or hearing.

## Practice

1. Al-Fatihah ends with الضَّالِّينَ (those who go astray) — the active participle of ضَلَال. How does this final word of the opening surah frame the entire Quran as a response to ضَلَال?
2. رِزْق in (51:22) is "in the heaven." How do classical commentators interpret this — is it literal or metaphorical? What does it say about reliance on God?
3. بَصِير and سَمِيع appear together dozens of times. Why do you think the Quran pairs these two divine attributes so consistently?

## Reflection

> "قُلْ إِنَّ هُدَى اللَّهِ هُوَ الْهُدَىٰ"
> "Say: Indeed, the guidance of Allah — that is the true guidance." (2:120)

This verse contains قُلْ (Lesson 3), إِنَّ (Lesson 2), هُوَ (Lesson 3), and هُدَى (this lesson). The repetition of هُدَى in a single short sentence — "the guidance of Allah is the guidance" — is a form of Quranic emphasis called تَوْكِيد (tawkid), underscoring that no other guidance is real guidance.
