# Lesson 3: Lord, Direction, and Command

## Words

- رَبّ (rabb) → Lord / Master / Sustainer — appears ~970 times
  Ayah: "الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ" — "All praise is for Allah, Lord of all worlds." (1:2)

- إِلَى (ila) → to / toward / until — appears ~742 times
  Ayah: "وَإِلَى اللَّهِ تُرْجَعُ الْأُمُورُ" — "And to Allah all matters are returned." (2:210)

- هُوَ (huwa) → he / it (3rd person masculine singular pronoun) — appears ~703 times
  Ayah: "قُلْ هُوَ اللَّهُ أَحَدٌ" — "Say: He is Allah, the One." (112:1)

- قُلْ (qul) → say! (2nd person masculine imperative) — appears ~332 times
  Ayah: "قُلْ أَعُوذُ بِرَبِّ النَّاسِ" — "Say: I seek refuge in the Lord of mankind." (114:1)

- عَنْ ('an) → from / about / away from / concerning — appears ~657 times
  Ayah: "وَاللَّهُ خَبِيرٌ بِمَا تَعْمَلُونَ" — "And Allah is Acquainted with what you do." (63:11) — note: عَنْ pairs with سَأَلَ: "يَسْأَلُونَكَ عَنِ الْأَهِلَّةِ" — "They ask you about the new moons." (2:189)

## Root Connection

- **رَبّ** comes from the root ر-ب-ب (r-b-b), meaning to nurture, raise, and sustain. It implies not just ownership but active care. Related: رَبَّى (he raised/nurtured), تَرْبِيَة (upbringing).
- **قُلْ** comes from the root ق-و-ل (q-w-l) — the same root as قَالَ (he said) from Lesson 2. قُلْ is the imperative: "You say!" It opens 332 divine commands to the Prophet.
- **هُوَ** is a standalone pronoun used for emphasis and theological declaration. In Surah Al-Ikhlas (112), هُوَ opens the definitive statement of God's oneness.
- **إِلَى** (to/toward) contrasts with **مِنْ** (from) learned in Lesson 1 — together they express origin and destination.

## Practice

1. Surah Al-Fatihah (1:2) uses رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ — what does the possessive form رَبِّ tell us grammatically?
2. Count how many surahs begin with قُلْ. What does this pattern tell us about the Quran's direct-address style?
3. Compare إِلَى and عَنْ as prepositions of direction — how do they indicate movement toward versus away from something?

## Reflection

> "قُلْ إِنَّ صَلَاتِي وَنُسُكِي وَمَحْيَايَ وَمَمَاتِي لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ"
> "Say: Indeed, my prayer, my rites, my living and my dying are for Allah, Lord of all worlds." (6:162)

This ayah contains both قُلْ and رَبّ from this lesson, and إِنَّ from Lesson 2 — see how these high-frequency words cluster in foundational declarations.
