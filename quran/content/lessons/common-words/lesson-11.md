# Lesson 11: Turning Back — Repentance, Gratitude, and Creation

## Words

- تَوْبَة (tawbah) → repentance / turning back (to God) — appears ~87 times (root occurrences)
  Ayah: "وَتُوبُوا إِلَى اللَّهِ جَمِيعًا أَيُّهَ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ" — "And turn to Allah in repentance, all of you, O believers." (24:31)

- شُكْر (shukr) → gratitude / thankfulness — appears ~75 times (root occurrences)
  Ayah: "لَئِن شَكَرْتُمْ لَأَزِيدَنَّكُمْ" — "If you are grateful, I will surely increase you." (14:7)

- دُعَاء (du'a') → supplication / calling upon / prayer — appears ~212 times (root occurrences)
  Ayah: "وَقَالَ رَبُّكُمُ ادْعُونِي أَسْتَجِبْ لَكُمْ" — "And your Lord says: Call upon Me; I will respond to you." (40:60)

- أَمْر (amr) → command / matter / affair — appears ~257 times
  Ayah: "أَلَا لَهُ الْخَلْقُ وَالْأَمْرُ" — "Unquestionably, His is the creation and the command." (7:54)

- خَلَقَ (khalaqa) → he created — appears ~261 times (root occurrences)
  Ayah: "خَلَقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ فِي سِتَّةِ أَيَّامٍ" — "He created the heavens and the earth in six days." (7:54)

## Root Connection

- **تَوْبَة** (root: ت-و-ب, t-w-b) → تَابَ (he repented), التَّوَّاب (the Acceptor of Repentance — a divine name). The root implies returning — تَوْبَة is the soul's U-turn back toward God. Paired with إِلَى (Lesson 3): تَابَ إِلَى اللَّه (he returned to Allah).
- **شُكْر** (root: ش-ك-ر, sh-k-r) → شَكَرَ (he was grateful), شَكُور (very grateful — both human and divine names), كُفْر (the opposite — ingratitude/disbelief, root ك-ف-ر from Lesson 8). Gratitude and disbelief are etymological antonyms in Arabic.
- **دُعَاء** (root: د-ع-و, d-'-w) → دَعَا (he called/supplicated), دَاعٍ (one who calls), مَدْعُوّ (the one called upon). The root covers calling, inviting, and naming. In (40:60), ادْعُونِي is the imperative plural: "Call upon Me!"
- **خَلَقَ** and **أَمْر** appear together in (7:54): creation (what God makes through physical process) and command (what God orders into existence with كُن — "Be!"). The verse نeatly separates these two modes of divine action.

## Practice

1. In (14:7), the promise is: gratitude leads to increase. What does شَكَرْتُمْ mean grammatically (who is speaking, who is addressed)?
2. أَمْر appears over 250 times with multiple meanings. In (7:54), it means divine command. Find another verse where it means "matter" or "affair" (hint: 2:210).
3. خَلَقَ is paired with السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ in (7:54) — a phrase also seen in Lessons 5 and 6. What does the Quran's repeated use of this pairing teach about creation?

## Reflection

> "فَاذْكُرُونِي أَذْكُرْكُمْ وَاشْكُرُوا لِي وَلَا تَكْفُرُونِ"
> "So remember Me; I will remember you. And be grateful to Me and do not deny Me." (2:152)

This verse contains ذِكْر (Lesson 8), شُكْر (this lesson), and لَا + كُفْر (Lessons 2 + 8) — a tight weave of interconnected concepts showing that remembrance, gratitude, and belief are inseparable.
