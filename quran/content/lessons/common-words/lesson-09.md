# Lesson 9: The Elements of Existence

## Words

- مَاء (ma') → water — appears ~63 times
  Ayah: "وَجَعَلْنَا مِنَ الْمَاءِ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ حَيٍّ" — "And We made from water every living thing." (21:30)

- مَوْت (mawt) → death — appears ~164 times
  Ayah: "كُلُّ نَفْسٍ ذَائِقَةُ الْمَوْتِ" — "Every soul will taste death." (3:185)

- حَيَاة (hayah) → life — appears ~76 times
  Ayah: "وَمَا الْحَيَاةُ الدُّنْيَا إِلَّا لَعِبٌ وَلَهْوٌ" — "And the worldly life is not but amusement and diversion." (6:32)

- جَنَّة (jannah) → paradise / garden — appears ~147 times
  Ayah: "وَبَشِّرِ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ أَنَّ لَهُمْ جَنَّاتٍ" — "And give glad tidings to those who believe and do righteous deeds that they will have gardens." (2:25)

- نَار (nar) → fire / hellfire — appears ~145 times
  Ayah: "وَاتَّقُوا النَّارَ الَّتِي أُعِدَّتْ لِلْكَافِرِينَ" — "And fear the Fire, which has been prepared for the disbelievers." (3:131)

## Root Connection

- **مَوْت** and **حَيَاة** come from opposite roots that define existence: م-و-ت (m-w-t, death) and ح-ي-ي (h-y-y, life). الْحَيّ (the Ever-Living) is a divine name. مَوْت and حَيَاة together appear dozens of times — the Quran consistently frames this life as a passage between two states.
- **مَاء** (root: م-و-ه or م-ي-ه) is the source of all biological life in the Quran (21:30). Spiritually, revelation is also compared to rain/water that revives the dead earth — a rich metaphor connecting this lesson's themes.
- **جَنَّة** (root: ج-ن-ن, j-n-n) → جَنَّ (to cover/conceal), جِنّ (jinn — hidden beings), جُنُون (madness — a "covered" mind). A garden (جَنَّة) is a lush, enclosed, shaded place. The root links hidden goodness.
- **نَار** (root: ن-و-ر, n-w-r) shares its root with نُور (light, Lesson 8). Fire gives light but also destroys — the same root captures both the illuminating and consuming dimensions of fire.

## Practice

1. How does (21:30) use مَاء to make a scientific and theological statement simultaneously?
2. The Quran pairs مَوْت and حَيَاة repeatedly. Find (67:2) where this pairing is used to explain the purpose of creation.
3. جَنَّة and نَار are often mentioned together as the two destinations. Find a verse that names both (hint: 3:131 names نَار; look for nearby context about جَنَّة).

## Reflection

> "يُحْيِي وَيُمِيتُ وَهُوَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ"
> "He gives life and causes death, and He is over all things competent." (57:2)

يُحْيِي (He gives life) and يُمِيتُ (He causes death) are the verb forms of حَيَاة and مَوْت. Combined with هُوَ (Lesson 3) and كُلِّ شَيْءٍ (Lesson 4 + 7), this verse weaves across six lessons of vocabulary.
