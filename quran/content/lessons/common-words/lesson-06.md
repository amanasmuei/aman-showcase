# Lesson 6: Scripture, Signs, and Community

## Words

- كِتَاب (kitab) → book / scripture / written record — appears ~261 times
  Ayah: "ذَٰلِكَ الْكِتَابُ لَا رَيْبَ فِيهِ هُدًى لِّلْمُتَّقِينَ" — "That is the Book about which there is no doubt, a guidance for those conscious of Allah." (2:2)

- آيَة (ayah) → sign / verse / miracle — appears ~382 times
  Ayah: "وَمِنْ آيَاتِهِ خَلْقُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ" — "And of His signs is the creation of the heavens and the earth." (30:22)

- قَوْم (qawm) → people / nation / tribe / community — appears ~383 times
  Ayah: "إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يُغَيِّرُ مَا بِقَوْمٍ حَتَّىٰ يُغَيِّرُوا مَا بِأَنفُسِهِمْ" — "Indeed, Allah will not change the condition of a people until they change what is in themselves." (13:11)

- عَذَاب ('adhab) → punishment / torment / suffering — appears ~373 times
  Ayah: "وَاتَّقُوا عَذَابًا لَّا يُصِيبَنَّ الَّذِينَ ظَلَمُوا مِنكُمْ خَاصَّةً" — "And fear a trial which will not strike those who have wronged among you exclusively." (8:25)

- رَحْمَة (rahmah) → mercy / compassion / grace — appears ~339 times
  Ayah: "وَرَحْمَتِي وَسِعَتْ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ" — "And My mercy encompasses all things." (7:156)

## Root Connection

- **كِتَاب** comes from ك-ت-ب (k-t-b) — to write. Related: كَتَبَ (he wrote), كَاتِب (writer), مَكْتُوب (written/destined). The Quran is itself الْكِتَاب — the written, preserved word.
- **آيَة** (root: أ-ي-ي / أ-و-ي) means a sign or marker. A verse of the Quran is an آيَة because each verse is a divine sign. The universe's phenomena are also called آيَات (signs of God).
- **رَحْمَة** (root: ر-ح-م, r-h-m) → رَحِيم (Most Merciful — a divine name), رَحْمَن (Most Gracious), رَحِمَ (he had mercy). This is the root of the Basmala and central to Islamic theology.
- **قَوْم** has no strong triliteral derivation in the classical sense but relates to قَامَ (to stand/rise — root ق-و-م). A قَوْم is those who "stand together" — a social unit. Contrast with نَاس (mankind generally) from Lesson 5.

## Practice

1. The word آيَة has two distinct meanings in the Quran: a Quranic verse and a cosmic sign. Find one example of each usage.
2. Surah Ar-Ra'd (13:11) uses قَوْم in a famous verse about change. What is the theological implication: does God change people, or do people change themselves?
3. رَحْمَة appears 339 times — find Allah's name الرَّحِيم in the opening of the Quran and trace the connection to this root.

## Reflection

> "كِتَابٌ أَنزَلْنَاهُ إِلَيْكَ لِتُخْرِجَ النَّاسَ مِنَ الظُّلُمَاتِ إِلَى النُّورِ"
> "A Book which We have revealed to you that you might bring mankind out of darkness into light." (14:1)

This verse contains كِتَاب (Lesson 6), النَّاسَ from Lesson 5, مِنَ and إِلَى from Lessons 1 and 3 — demonstrating how foundational vocabulary builds toward complex meaning.
