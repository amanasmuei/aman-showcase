# Lesson 2: Negation, Assertion, and Speech

## Words

- لَا (la) → no / not (negation) — appears ~1738 times
  Ayah: "لَا إِكْرَاهَ فِي الدِّينِ" — "There is no compulsion in religion." (2:256)

- إِنَّ (inna) → indeed / verily / truly (emphasis particle) — appears ~1531 times
  Ayah: "إِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلَى كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ" — "Indeed, Allah is over all things competent." (2:20)

- عَلَى ('ala) → upon / on / over — appears ~1445 times
  Ayah: "وَعَلَى اللَّهِ فَتَوَكَّلُوا" — "And upon Allah rely." (5:23)

- كَانَ (kana) → was / were / has been — appears ~1358 times
  Ayah: "وَكَانَ اللَّهُ غَفُورًا رَّحِيمًا" — "And Allah is ever Forgiving and Merciful." (4:96)

- قَالَ (qala) → he said / he spoke — appears ~529 times
  Ayah: "قَالَ رَبِّ اشْرَحْ لِي صَدْرِي" — "He said: My Lord, expand for me my chest." (20:25)

## Root Connection

- **كَانَ** comes from the root ك-و-ن (k-w-n), meaning "to be / to exist." This root also produces كُنْ (kun — "Be!"), كَوْن (existence/universe), and يَكُونُ (yakunu — it will be).
- **قَالَ** comes from the root ق-و-ل (q-w-l), meaning "to say / speech." Related words: قَوْل (qawl — saying), يَقُولُ (yaqulu — he says), قِيلَ (qila — it was said).
- **إِنَّ** and **لَا** are particles. إِنَّ opens many Quranic statements of truth; لَا is the bedrock of Tawhid in لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللَّه.
- **عَلَى** is a preposition of elevation — things resting "upon" or authority "over." Compare with فِي (in) from Lesson 1.

## Practice

1. Translate the phrase: "إِنَّ اللَّهَ كَانَ غَفُورًا رَّحِيمًا" — what tense does كَانَ suggest here?
2. In the ayah from Surah Al-Baqarah (2:256), identify لَا and explain how it creates an absolute statement.
3. How does قَالَ signal the beginning of direct speech in Quranic narrative? Find the example from Surah Ta-Ha (20:25).

## Reflection

> "إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يَظْلِمُ النَّاسَ شَيْئًا"
> "Indeed, Allah does not wrong the people at all." (10:44)

This verse uses إِنَّ for emphasis and لَا for negation — two of this lesson's core words working together to make a definitive statement about divine justice.
