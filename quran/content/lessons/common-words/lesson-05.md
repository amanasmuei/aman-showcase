# Lesson 5: Faith, People, and the World Above

## Words

- آمَنَ (amana) → he believed / he had faith — appears ~537 times (verb form)
  Ayah: "آمَنَ الرَّسُولُ بِمَا أُنزِلَ إِلَيْهِ مِن رَّبِّهِ" — "The Messenger has believed in what was revealed to him from his Lord." (2:285)

- عَمِلَ ('amila) → he did / he worked / he acted — appears ~360 times (verb form)
  Ayah: "مَنْ عَمِلَ صَالِحًا فَلِنَفْسِهِ" — "Whoever does righteousness — it is for his own soul." (41:46)

- نَاس (nas) → people / mankind / humankind — appears ~241 times
  Ayah: "يَا أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ اتَّقُوا رَبَّكُمُ" — "O mankind, fear your Lord." (4:1)

- سَمَاء (sama') → sky / heaven — appears ~310 times
  Ayah: "وَفِي السَّمَاءِ رِزْقُكُمْ وَمَا تُوعَدُونَ" — "And in the heaven is your provision and whatever you are promised." (51:22)

- عِلْم ('ilm) → knowledge / knowing — appears ~854 times (root form)
  Ayah: "وَفَوْقَ كُلِّ ذِي عِلْمٍ عَلِيمٌ" — "And above every possessor of knowledge is one more knowing." (12:76)

## Root Connection

- **آمَنَ** and the word أَمْن (security/safety) share the root أ-م-ن (a-m-n). Faith in Arabic is إِيمَان (iman), and a believer is مُؤْمِن (mu'min — Lesson 8). The root conveys both trust and security.
- **عَمِلَ** (root: ع-م-ل, '-m-l) → عَمَل (deed/work), عَامِل (worker), أَعْمَال (deeds/acts). The Quran pairs آمَنَ and عَمِلَ together hundreds of times: "those who believe and do righteous deeds."
- **عِلْم** (root: ع-ل-م, '-l-m) → عَالِم (scholar/knower), عَلِيم (All-Knowing — one of Allah's names), مَعْلُوم (known). The root '-l-m is one of the most productive roots in Arabic.
- **نَاس** and **سَمَاء** lack shared roots with each other, but سَمَاء (sky/heaven) often pairs with أَرْض (earth) from Lesson 4 — above and below, the full created universe.

## Practice

1. The Quran repeatedly pairs آمَنَ with عَمِلَ — find a verse where both appear together (hint: 2:277) and explain the relationship between faith and action this pairing implies.
2. How many times does يَا أَيُّهَا النَّاسُ ("O mankind!") appear in the Quran? What does this address say about the Quran's universal audience?
3. In (12:76), عِلْم appears in the phrase "ذِي عِلْمٍ" (possessor of knowledge). What does this grammatical construction tell you about how عِلْم is used?

## Reflection

> "الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ وَآمَنُوا بِمَا نُزِّلَ عَلَىٰ مُحَمَّدٍ"
> "Those who believe and do righteous deeds and believe in what was sent down upon Muhammad." (47:2)

This verse contains آمَنُوا (the plural of آمَنَ) and عَمِلُوا (the plural of عَمِلَ) — the Quran's most iconic pairing, showing that belief without action is incomplete.
