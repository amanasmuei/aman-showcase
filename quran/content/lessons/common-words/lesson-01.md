# Lesson 1: The Most Essential Words

## Words

- اللَّه (Allah) → God / Allah — appears ~2699 times
  Ayah: "بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ" — "In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful." (1:1)

- مِنْ (min) → from / out of — appears ~3226 times
  Ayah: "خَلَقَ الْإِنسَانَ مِن عَلَقٍ" — "He created man from a clinging substance." (96:2)

- مَا (ma) → what / that which / not — appears ~2076 times
  Ayah: "وَمَا خَلَقْتُ الْجِنَّ وَالْإِنسَ إِلَّا لِيَعْبُدُونِ" — "And I did not create the jinn and mankind except to worship Me." (51:56)

- فِي (fi) → in / within — appears ~1701 times
  Ayah: "وَهُوَ مَعَكُمْ أَيْنَ مَا كُنتُمْ" — "And He is with you wherever you are." (57:4)

- الَّذِي (alladhi) → who / which / that (relative pronoun) — appears ~1464 times
  Ayah: "الَّذِي خَلَقَ الْمَوْتَ وَالْحَيَاةَ" — "Who created death and life." (67:2)

## Root Connection

- **اللَّه** has no derivational root in the usual sense — it is the proper name of God, though some scholars link it to the root أ-ل-ه (alaha, to worship/deity).
- **مِنْ** and **فِي** are particles with no trilateral root — they are function words that glue meaning together.
- **مَا** serves double duty: as a relative pronoun ("what/that which") and as a negation particle ("not"). Watch the context carefully.
- **الَّذِي** is the masculine singular relative pronoun. Its feminine form is الَّتِي (allati) and its plural is الَّذِينَ (alladhina) — all extremely frequent.

## Practice

1. Translate: "مِنَ اللَّهِ" — what does this phrase mean, and in what context might it appear?
2. In the ayah from Surah Al-'Alaq (96:2), identify the word مِنْ and explain what it connects.
3. How does مَا function differently in 51:56 compared to its use as a negation particle?

## Reflection

> "اللَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْحَيُّ الْقَيُّومُ"
> "Allah — there is no deity except Him, the Ever-Living, the Self-Sustaining." (2:255)

This single ayah (Ayat al-Kursi) contains اللَّه and مَا's negation cousin لَا — notice how these foundational words combine to express absolute monotheism.
