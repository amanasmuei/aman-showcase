# Lesson 4: Time, Totality, and Choice

## Words

- يَوْم (yawm) → day / the Day (esp. Day of Judgment) — appears ~475 times
  Ayah: "مَالِكِ يَوْمِ الدِّينِ" — "Master of the Day of Recompense." (1:4)

- أَنَّ (anna) → that / because (conjunction introducing a subordinate clause) — appears ~1523 times
  Ayah: "أَلَمْ يَعْلَمُوا أَنَّ اللَّهَ هُوَ يَقْبَلُ التَّوْبَةَ" — "Did they not know that it is Allah who accepts repentance?" (9:104)

- كُلّ (kull) → every / all / each — appears ~537 times
  Ayah: "كُلُّ نَفْسٍ ذَائِقَةُ الْمَوْتِ" — "Every soul will taste death." (3:185)

- أَرْض (ard) → earth / land / ground — appears ~461 times
  Ayah: "لِلَّهِ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ" — "To Allah belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is in the earth." (2:284)

- أَوْ (aw) → or / either…or (disjunction) — appears ~640 times
  Ayah: "فَكَفَّارَتُهُ إِطْعَامُ عَشَرَةِ مَسَاكِينَ أَوْ كِسْوَتُهُمْ" — "Its expiation is feeding ten poor persons or clothing them." (5:89)

## Root Connection

- **يَوْم** (root: ي-و-م, y-w-m) is used for ordinary days and, with the definite article, often refers to يَوْمُ الْقِيَامَة (the Day of Resurrection). Its plural is أَيَّام (ayyam — days).
- **أَرْض** (root: أ-ر-ض, '-r-d) means earth/land. Its frequent pairing with السَّمَاوَات (the heavens) forms a Quranic formula for "all of creation."
- **كُلّ** has the root ك-ل-ل (k-l-l). It universalizes — كُلُّ نَفْس means "without exception, every single soul." When followed by a definite noun, it means "all of"; with indefinite, "every."
- **أَنَّ** is the "open" or "fused" form of the emphasis particle إِنَّ (Lesson 2). After verbs of knowing, saying, or thinking, إِنَّ becomes أَنَّ in embedded clauses.

## Practice

1. In (3:185) "كُلُّ نَفْسٍ ذَائِقَةُ الْمَوْتِ" — what grammatical case is نَفْسٍ, and why?
2. The word يَوْم appears in Surah Al-Fatihah — look up its full context. How does this set the tone for the entire Quran?
3. How does أَنَّ in (9:104) differ functionally from إِنَّ in Lesson 2 examples?

## Reflection

> "يَوْمَ لَا تَمْلِكُ نَفْسٌ لِنَفْسٍ شَيْئًا وَالْأَمْرُ يَوْمَئِذٍ لِلَّهِ"
> "The Day when no soul will avail another soul at all, and the command that Day is with Allah." (82:19)

This verse features يَوْم twice, reinforcing its weight. Notice also كُلّ's implicit shadow — the totality of every soul on that Day.
