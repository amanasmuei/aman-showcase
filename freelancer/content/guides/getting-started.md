# Getting Started as a Freelancer

A practical guide for those just starting out or looking to improve their freelance business.

## Setting Up Your Business

### 1. Define Your Service
- What do you do? Be specific: "I build responsive websites" not "I do tech stuff"
- Who is your ideal client? Industry, size, budget
- What makes you different? Speed, quality, niche expertise, personality

### 2. Set Your Rates
- Research market rates for your skill and experience level
- Calculate your minimum: (monthly expenses + taxes + savings) / billable hours per month
- The billable hours trap: you won't bill 40 hours/week. Realistically 20-25 hours are billable.
- Start competitively, increase as you build reputation and demand

### 3. Create Your Basics
- Portfolio: 3-5 best work samples (quality over quantity)
- Simple contract template: scope, timeline, payment, revisions, cancellation
- Invoice template: professional, numbered, clear payment instructions
- Professional email: yourname@yourdomain.com (not gmail for client communication)

## Finding Clients

### Channels That Work
- Referrals: tell everyone you know. #1 source for most freelancers.
- LinkedIn: optimize profile, share work, engage with potential clients
- Upwork/Fiverr: good for building early reputation, not long-term strategy
- Cold outreach: research companies that need your service, send personalized emails
- Content: blog posts, tutorials, case studies that showcase your expertise

### What Doesn't Work
- Waiting for clients to find you
- Competing on price alone
- Sending generic proposals
- Not following up (80% of deals happen after the 5th follow-up)

## Managing Your Business

### Weekly Routine
- Monday: review pipeline, send follow-ups, plan the week
- Daily: track time, update project status, respond to clients
- Friday: send invoices for completed work, review finances

### Monthly Routine
- Send all outstanding invoices
- Follow up on overdue payments
- Review income vs expenses
- Update portfolio with recent work
- Evaluate: which clients are profitable? Which drain energy?

### Financial Basics
- Save 25-30% of income for taxes (varies by country)
- Keep business and personal finances separate
- Track every expense (software, equipment, coworking, travel)
- Build 3-month runway: enough savings to cover 3 months of expenses with zero income
- Invoice immediately — don't wait

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
1. Working without a contract — always get it in writing
2. Undercharging — factor in taxes, expenses, non-billable time, health insurance
3. Saying yes to everything — bad clients cost more than no clients
4. Not tracking time — even for fixed-price projects, know your effective rate
5. Ignoring finances — check your numbers weekly, not yearly
6. Client concentration — if one client is 50%+ of income, diversify immediately
7. No boundaries — set working hours, response times, and revision limits
